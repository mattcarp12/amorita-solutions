Hello, my name is Matthew Carpenter. I'm a senior software engineer based in the Boise, Idaho area with over a decade of
experience building backend systems, cloud infrastructure, data pipelines, and
distributed platforms. I have a broad technical background and a track record of
picking up new domains quickly — whether that's embedded IoT firmware, machine
learning pipelines, or Kubernetes-native tooling.

Before software engineering, I studied Applied Mathematics at UC Berkeley and worked
as a pricing analyst in the insurance industry, where I built
predictive models and worked with large datasets. I also hold ACAS and CSPA
actuarial designations. That analytical foundation shows up in how I approach engineering
problems — careful about data, comfortable with ambiguity, and focused on getting the
model right before optimizing the implementation.

I served four years in the United States Marine Corps, including
combat deployments to Iraq and Afghanistan. That experience shaped how I work on teams:
directly, with a bias toward action, and with a clear sense of what actually matters under pressure.

Designed data storage and retrieval software for embedded IoT device fleets. Built

and IAM. Architected CI/CD pipelines with GitHub Actions and led technical hiring.

Developed backend systems in Go for Comcast's customer-facing ISP speed test platform.

**Intuit** — Software Engineer _(2023)_
Contributed to an internal incident management and alerting platform with integrations
into Slack, Zoom, and AWS event-driven workflows.
